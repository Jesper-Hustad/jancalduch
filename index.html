What is an embedded system?

An embedded system is a computer system that is designed to perform a specific task or tasks. Embedded systems are found in a wide range of devices, from cell phones and MP3 players to industrial robots and aircraft.

Embedded systems are usually designed to be small and efficient, and to use as little power as possible. This is because they are often used in portable devices, or in devices that need to run for a long time without being turned off.

Embedded systems are often designed to be very reliable, because they are often used in critical applications. For example, an embedded system might be used to control the brakes of a car, or to control the systems of a nuclear power plant.

What do embedded systems designers do?

Embedded systems designers are responsible for designing and developing the software and hardware for embedded systems. This includes choosing the right microprocessor and designing the circuits that the system will use.

Embedded systems designers also need to be familiar with the operating systems that are commonly used in embedded systems, such as Linux and RTOS. They also need to know how to develop software in C and C++.

In addition to their technical skills, embedded systems designers also need to have good problem-solving skills. This is because they often need to debug embedded systems that are not working correctly.
